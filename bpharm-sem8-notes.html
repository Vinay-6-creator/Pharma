Advanced Drug Delivery Systems

•
Nanotechnology: Nanoparticles, liposomes, micelles in drug delivery
•
Targeted Delivery: Ligand-based targeting, antibody conjugates
•
Modified Release: Extended-release, sustained-release formulations

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Discuss advantages of nanoparticle-based drug delivery
Answer: Enhanced solubility of poorly soluble drugs, targeted delivery to specific tissues, reduced side effects, prolonged circulation time, better cellular uptake, biodegradable options available.
II

Biopharmaceuticals & Biotechnology Drugs

•
Recombinant Proteins: Insulin, interferons, growth factors production
•
Monoclonal Antibodies: Therapeutic antibodies, humanized versions
•
Gene Therapy: Gene editing, CAR-T cells, CRISPR applications

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Explain how monoclonal antibodies are produced and their therapeutic applications
Answer: Produced using hybridoma technology or recombinant DNA methods. Applications: cancer immunotherapy (trastuzumab), autoimmune diseases (infliximab), inflammation (adalimumab) management.

Pharmacy Practice & Professional Development

•
Pharmacy Roles: Clinical pharmacy, hospital pharmacy, community pharmacy responsibilities
•
Business Management: Inventory, financial management, pricing strategies
•

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Discuss the role of clinical pharmacist in hospital settings
Answer: Drug therapy optimization, adverse effect monitoring, dosage adjustment, drug interaction prevention, patient education, clinical rounds participation, formulary management, quality assurance.
IV

Emerging Trends & Future of Pharmacy

•
Personalized Medicine: Pharmacogenomics, biomarker-guided therapy
•
Digital Health: Telepharmacy, AI in drug discovery, e-health integration
•
Sustainability: Green pharmacy, waste management, environmental responsibility

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. What is pharmacogenomics and its clinical significance?
Answer: Study of genetic factors affecting drug response. Enables personalized dosing (e.g., warfarin CYP2C9 variants), predicts adverse reactions, improves therapeutic outcomes by tailoring treatments to individual genetics.
