BP101T
Human Anatomy and Physiology I
Credits: 4
L-T: 3-1
Hours: 45
🎯 Course Outcomes:
  1. Explain anatomical and physiological concepts of human body systems
  2. Describe homeostasis and its mechanisms
  3. Apply anatomical knowledge in pharmaceutical practice
📖 Key Topics:
  • Cell biology: structure, function, cell division
  • Tissues: epithelial, connective, muscle, nervous
  • Integumentary system
  • Skeletal system: bones and joints
  • Muscular system: types and contraction
  • Cardiovascular system: heart and blood vessels
  • Respiratory system: anatomy and gas exchange
  • Nervous system: CNS, PNS, and reflex arc
  • Sensory organs: eye, ear, taste, smell
BP102T
Pharmaceutical Analysis I
Credits: 4
L-T: 3-1
Hours: 45
🎯 Course Outcomes:
  1. Explain principles of pharmaceutical analysis
  2. Apply analytical techniques for quality control
  3. Interpret analytical data
📖 Key Topics:
  • Gravimetric analysis principles
  • Volumetric analysis and titrimetry
  • Acid-base titrations
  • Redox titrations
  • Complexometric titrations (EDTA)
  • Precipitation titrations
  • Official analysis methods: IP, BP, USP
  • Quality control applications
BP103T
Pharmaceutics I
Credits: 4
L-T: 3-1
Hours: 45
📖 Key Topics:
  • Pharmaceutical processes: size reduction, mixing
  • Particle size and distribution
  • Powders: properties and flow
  • Granulation: wet and dry methods
  • Drying principles and equipment
  • Extraction methods
  • Filtration and sterilization
  • GMP fundamentals
BP104T
Pharmaceutical Inorganic Chemistry
Credits: 4
L-T: 3-1
Hours: 45
📖 Key Topics:
  • Atomic structure and electronic configuration
  • Chemical bonding: ionic, covalent
  • Periodic table and trends
  • Inorganic pharmaceuticals
  • Alkalies and alkaline earth metals
  • Halogens and pharmaceutical applications
  • Complexes and coordination compounds
  • Dosage forms of inorganic drugs
BP105T
Communication Skills

L: 2
Hours: 30
📖 Key Topics:
  • Communication fundamentals and barriers
  • Professional communication
  • Written communication skills
  • Oral presentation and public speaking
  • Interpersonal communication
  • Patient counselling techniques
  • Digital communication

SEMESTER II
30 Credits

📚 Courses: Human Anatomy and Physiology II, Pharmaceutical Analysis II, Pharmaceutics II, Pharmaceutical Organic Chemistry I, Biochemistry

Key Features:

  • Advanced spectroscopic methods (UV-Visible, Fluorimetry)
  • Chromatography: TLC, Paper, Gas Chromatography
  • Introduction to HPLC
  • Semi-solid dosage forms: ointments, creams, gels
  • Organic chemistry: nomenclature, isomerism, reactions
  • Biochemistry: metabolic pathways, enzyme kinetics
🔬 5 PRACTICAL COURSES

BP206P - BP210P covering analytical, biochemical, and formulation practicals

SEMESTER III
30 Credits

📚 Courses: Pathophysiology, Pharmaceutical Organic Chemistry II, Biochemistry II, Pharmacology I, Social Pharmacy

Key Features:

  • Disease mechanisms and pathophysiology
  • Heterocyclic compounds and drug synthesis
  • Advanced enzyme kinetics and signal transduction
  • Pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics fundamentals
  • Autonomic nervous system pharmacology
  • Healthcare systems and epidemiology
SEMESTER IV
30 Credits

📚 Courses: Medicinal Chemistry I, Pharmacology II, Pharmaceutics III, Pharmacognosy I, Pharmaceutical Jurisprudence

Key Features:

  • Structure-Activity Relationships (SAR)
  • Drug metabolism: Phase I, II, III reactions
  • Solid dosage forms: tablets, capsules
  • Natural products and phytochemistry
  • Pharmaceutical laws and regulations
  • Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)
SEMESTER V
30 Credits

📚 Courses: Pharmacology III, Medicinal Chemistry II, Pharmacognosy II, Pharmaceutical Microbiology, Advanced Analytical Methods

Key Features:

  • CNS pharmacology and psychotropic drugs
  • Drug metabolism and bioactivation
  • Medicinal plants and herbal medicines
  • Microbiological techniques and sterilization
  • HPLC, LC-MS, GC-MS methods
  • Analytical method validation (ICH Q2)
SEMESTER VI
30 Credits

📚 Courses: Biopharmaceutics & Pharmacokinetics, Clinical Pharmacy, Pharmaceutics IV (NDDS), Pharmacology IV, Advanced Formulation Design

Key Features:

  • Bioavailability and bioequivalence
  • Pharmacokinetic models and dosing
  • Clinical pharmacy and therapeutic management
  • Novel drug delivery systems (NDDS)
  • Liposomes, nanoparticles, hydrogels
  • Quality by Design (QbD) principles
SEMESTER VII
22 Credits

📚 Courses: Social Pharmacy, Pharmaceutical Biotechnology, Research & Development, Hospital/Clinical Pharmacy, Regulatory Affairs

Key Features:

  • Public health and health promotion
  • Biotechnology: cell culture, recombinant DNA
  • Drug discovery and development
  • Clinical trials and GCP guidelines
  • Hospital pharmacy operations
  • Regulatory compliance and GMP
  • Research projects: 6 Credits
  • Advanced elective courses available
SEMESTER VIII (FINAL)
22-24 Credits

📚 Courses: AI in Pharmacy, Clinical Pharmacotherapeutics, Industrial Pharmacy, Management, Sterile Dosage Forms

Key Features - NEW (2026 UPDATES):

  • 🤖 Artificial Intelligence in Pharmacy: AI lifecycle, ML models, ethical considerations
  • 📊 Pharma 4.0 Technologies: AR/VR, digital twins, automation
  • 🏥 Clinical Pharmacotherapeutics: Disease management, drug therapy
  • 🏭 Industrial Pharmacy: Facility design, technology transfer
  • 🎯 Precision Medicine: Pharmacogenomics, personalized therapy
  • 💊 Sterile & Novel Delivery: Parenteral, NDDS, advanced systems
  • 📚 Research Project: 6 Credits - Independent research work
🔬 ELECTIVE COURSES (Choose One Each)
  • Advanced Electives: Pharmaceutical Packaging, Supply Chain Management, Industrial Safety, Traditional Healing, AR/VR Pharma 4.0, Herbal Cosmetics
  • Validation Courses: Cleaning Validation, Aseptic Handling, Impurity Profiling
