Pharmaceutical Formulation & Technology

Formulation principles, processing methods, and manufacturing of dosage forms.

I

Dosage Form Fundamentals

•
Solid Dosage Forms: Tablets, capsules, powders, granules classification
•
Liquid Dosage Forms: Solutions, suspensions, emulsions principles
•
Semi-solid Dosage Forms: Ointments, creams, gels formulation

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Differentiate between suspension and emulsion
Answer: Suspension: Insoluble solid particles in liquid (e.g., antacid suspension). Emulsion: Two immiscible liquids stabilized together (e.g., oil-in-water or water-in-oil)
II

Tablet Technology

•
Tablet Excipients: Binders, diluents, disintegrants, lubricants
•
Tablet Manufacturing: Wet granulation, dry granulation processes
•
Tablet Compression: Machine parameters, compression problems and solutions

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Explain why disintegrants are added to tablets
Answer: Disintegrants break down tablets into smaller particles upon contact with water/GI fluids, facilitating drug dissolution and absorption. Examples: Croscarmellose sodium, sodium starch glycolate.
III

Capsules & Other Dosage Forms

•
Capsule Types: Hard gelatin, soft gelatin, HPMC capsules
•
Capsule Filling: Manual and machine filling methods
•
Powder Formulations: Bulk powders, divided powders manufacturing

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Why are HPMC capsules preferred over gelatin capsules?
Answer: HPMC capsules are vegetarian, stable at higher humidity, compatible with moisture-sensitive drugs, and not affected by formaldehyde cross-linking.
IV

Quality Control in Formulation

•
Tablet Testing: Hardness, friability, dissolution tests
•
Content Uniformity: Weight variation, content assay
•
Stability Testing: ICH guidelines, accelerated stability studies

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. What is dissolution testing and its significance?
Answer: Dissolution testing measures the rate and extent of drug release from dosage form in simulated GI fluids. It correlates in vitro release with in vivo bioavailability.
