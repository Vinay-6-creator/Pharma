Case 1: Drug Dosage Adjustment in Renal Failure

Pharmacokinetics
⭐ Medium
A 65-year-old male patient with chronic kidney disease (Stage 4) presents with hypertension. How should you adjust the dosage of Lisinopril (ACE inhibitor) for this patient?
Patient Details:
• Age: 65 years old
• Condition: Stage 4 Chronic Kidney Disease (GFR: 25 ml/min)
• Current Medication: Lisinopril 10mg daily
• BP: 160/95 mmHg
• Other: Diabetes, Hyperuricemia
Key Questions:
• How does renal function affect drug clearance?
• Should the dose be reduced or interval increased?
• What are the risks of not adjusting the dose?
• Are there alternative antihypertensives better suited?

Case 2: Drug-Drug Interaction Management

Elderly Patient on Multiple Medications

🔥 Hard
A 72-year-old female on Warfarin therapy for atrial fibrillation is prescribed Amoxicillin for a UTI. What are the potential interactions and how would you manage this?
Clinical Scenario:
• Warfarin 5mg daily (INR: 2.5)
• Amoxicillin 500mg TDS prescribed
• Also taking: Metformin, Atorvastatin, Aspirin
• Concerns: Increased bleeding risk
Discussion Topics:
• Mechanism of Warfarin-Antibiotic interaction
• Should Amoxicillin be substituted?
• How often should INR be monitored?
• What patient counseling is needed?

Case 3: Side Effects vs. Disease Progression

Clinical Practice
✨ Easy
A patient on Metformin therapy reports increased gastrointestinal symptoms. How would you differentiate between medication side effects and disease-related complications?
Patient Presentation:
• Metformin 1500mg daily for Type 2 Diabetes
• Symptoms: Nausea, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort
• Duration: Last 2 weeks
• Blood glucose: Well controlled
Learn About:
• Metformin side effect profile
• Strategies to minimize GI effects
• When to consider medication changes
• Patient compliance and persistence
