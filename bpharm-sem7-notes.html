❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Explain why combination chemotherapy is preferred over monotherapy
Answer: Combinations target different cell cycle phases, reduce resistance development, allow dose reduction with less toxicity, and improve response rates significantly.
II

•
Antidepressants: SSRIs, SNRIs, tricyclic agents mechanism and use
•
Antipsychotics: Typical vs atypical, extrapyramidal side effects
•
Neurological Disorders: Epilepsy, Parkinson's, Alzheimer's management

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Compare atypical antipsychotics with typical antipsychotics
Answer: Atypical: Better efficacy for negative symptoms, fewer extrapyramidal effects but weight gain/metabolic issues. Typical: Potential tardive dyskinesia, more EPSEs but lower metabolic risk.

•
ADR Monitoring: Types, classification, reporting systems

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. What is a yellow card system in pharmacovigilance?
Answer: System for healthcare professionals to report suspected ADRs to regulatory authorities. Helps identify new safety signals and assess risk-benefit profiles post-launch.
IV

Evidence-Based Practice & Research

•
Clinical Trial Design: Phase I, II, III, IV, randomization blinding
•
Statistical Analysis: P-values, confidence intervals, sample size calculations
•
EBM Hierarchy: RCTs, meta-analysis, systematic reviews interpretation

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Why are randomized controlled trials considered gold standard in clinical research?
Answer: Randomization eliminates selection bias, blinding prevents observer/patient bias, and control groups provide baseline comparison. This design provides strongest evidence for causation.
