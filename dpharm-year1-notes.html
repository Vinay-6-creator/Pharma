Pharmaceutical Calculations & Pharmacology Basics

Essential calculations for pharmacy and fundamental pharmacology principles.

I

Pharmaceutical Calculations

•
Metric Conversions: Weight, volume, temperature unit conversions
•
Percentage Calculations: w/w, w/v, v/v strength determinations
•
Dosage Calculations: Age-based, weight-based dose determinations

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Convert 250 mg to grams
Answer: 250 mg = 250 ÷ 1000 = 0.25 g
II

Pharmacology Introduction

•
Drug Routes: Oral, IV, IM, topical routes of administration
•
Drug Actions: Agonists, antagonists, side effects classification
•
Common Drug Classes: Analgesics, antibiotics, antihistamines uses

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Differentiate between agonist and antagonist drugs
Answer: Agonist: Binds to receptor and produces response (e.g., morphine). Antagonist: Binds to receptor but produces no response, blocks agonist action (e.g., naloxone).
III

Prescription Handling

•
Prescription Components: 5 Rs check (Right drug, dose, patient, route, time)
•
Drug Interactions: Identification and counseling on interactions
•
Dispensing Ethics: Professional responsibility and regulations

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Explain the 5 Rs of prescription verification
Answer: Right Patient, Right Drug, Right Dose, Right Route, Right Time - verification ensures patient safety and prevents medication errors.
IV

Over-the-Counter (OTC) Products

•
Common OTC Drugs: Analgesics, antacids, cough syrups usage
•
Patient Counseling: Proper use, warnings, when to seek physician
•
Adverse Effects: Recognition and appropriate action

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. What counseling should be provided for paracetamol use?
Answer: Correct dosage (max 4g/day), avoid overdose risk (hepatotoxicity), don't combine with other acetaminophen products, signs of overdose, when to seek medical help.
