B Pharm - Semester 4

Medicinal Chemistry

Structure-activity relationships, drug design principles, and synthesis of important therapeutic agents.

I

Drug Design Principles & SAR

•
Structure-Activity Relationship (SAR): How molecular structure affects drug activity
•
Lead Compound Selection: Identification and optimization of drug candidates
•
Molecular Modification: Isosteric replacement, bioisosteres in drug design

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Explain what is a bioisostere and give pharmaceutical examples
Answer: Bioisosteres are atoms/groups with similar size and chemical properties that can be substituted to improve drug properties. Example: -OH replaced by -NH₂ in various drugs while maintaining activity.
II

Quantitative Structure-Activity Relationships

•
QSAR Models: Hansch equation, Free-Wilson model
•
Lipophilicity: LogP, partition coefficient and drug absorption
•
Electronic Effects: Hammett constants, Taft's steric constants

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. What is LogP and how does it affect drug bioavailability?
Answer: LogP is octanol-water partition coefficient. Optimal LogP (2-5) ensures good membrane permeability and absorption. Too high LogP decreases solubility; too low decreases membrane penetration.
III

Pharmaceutical Analysis Methods

•
Qualitative Analysis: Identification tests, physical methods
•
Quantitative Analysis: Titrimetry, gravimetry, instrumental methods
•
Chromatographic Techniques: TLC, HPLC, GC principles

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Explain the principle and application of HPLC in drug analysis
Answer: HPLC separates components based on different affinities for mobile and stationary phases. Applications: purity testing, content determination, impurity analysis of pharmaceuticals.
IV

Important Drug Classes & Synthesis

•
Analgesics & NSAIDs: Aspirin, paracetamol, ibuprofen synthesis and mechanism
•
Antibiotics: Penicillins, cephalosporins structure and activity
•
Antihistamines: H₁ and H₂ receptor antagonists

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Why is the β-lactam ring essential for antibiotic activity of penicillins?
Answer: β-lactam ring forms covalent bonds with penicillin-binding proteins, inhibiting bacterial cell wall synthesis. Loss of this ring results in loss of antibiotic activity.
