❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Explain the role of ACE inhibitors in heart failure management
Answer: ACE inhibitors inhibit angiotensin II formation, reducing vasoconstriction and aldosterone secretion. This decreases preload/afterload, improves cardiac output, and prevents ventricular remodeling.
II

GI & Endocrine Pharmacotherapy

•
Peptic Ulcer Disease: PPI therapy, H2 receptor antagonists
•
Diabetes Management: Insulin, oral hypoglycemic agents
•
Thyroid Disorders: Thyroid replacement, antithyroid drugs

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Compare sulfonylureas and biguanides in diabetes treatment
Answer: Sulfonylureas stimulate insulin release (risk of hypoglycemia, weight gain). Biguanides reduce glucose production and increase insulin sensitivity (no hypoglycemia, weight neutral).
III

•
Immunomodulating Drugs: Corticosteroids, immunosuppressants
•
Antiinfectives: Antibiotics selection, resistance management
•
Antiretrovirals: HIV management, combination therapy principles

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Explain antibiotic resistance and strategies to prevent it
Answer: Resistance develops through mutations/horizontal gene transfer. Prevention: appropriate antibiotic selection, complete course adherence, narrow-spectrum agents, infection control measures.
IV

•
Counseling Skills: Communication, empathy, active listening

Drug Information: Side effects, drug interactions, dosage counseling
•
Medication Adherence: Strategies to improve patient compliance

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. What information should be counseled when dispensing metformin?
Answer: Take with meals to reduce GI upset, avoid alcohol, report signs of lactic acidosis, maintain regular glucose monitoring, dietary compliance importance, missed dose instructions.
