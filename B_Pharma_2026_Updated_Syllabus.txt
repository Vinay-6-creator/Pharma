🎯 PROGRAM STRUCTURE:
- Duration: 8 Semesters (4 Academic Years)
- Total Credits: 208
- Board: Pharmacy Council of India (PCI)
- Minimum Attendance: 80% in each course
- Medium of Instruction: English
- Implementation: NEP 2020 Framework

=====================================================================
SEMESTER I (Odd Semester - June/July to November/December)
=====================================================================

📖 COURSE STRUCTURE: 27-30 Total Credits

1. BP101T - HUMAN ANATOMY AND PHYSIOLOGY I (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Explain anatomical and physiological concepts of human body systems
   • Describe homeostasis and its mechanisms
   • Apply anatomical knowledge in pharmaceutical practice
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Introduction to anatomy and physiology, homeostasis concept
   • Cell biology: structure, function, cell division
   • Tissues: epithelial, connective, muscle, nervous tissues
   • Integumentary system: structure and functions
   • Skeletal system: bones, joints, skeletal anatomy
   • Muscular system: types of muscles, muscle contraction
   • Cardiovascular system: heart anatomy, blood vessels, circulation
   • Respiratory system: anatomy, gas exchange, breathing mechanics
   • Nervous system: CNS, PNS, neuronal structure, reflex arc
   • Sensory organs: eye, ear, taste, smell receptors

2. BP102T - PHARMACEUTICAL ANALYSIS I (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Explain principles of pharmaceutical analysis
   • Apply analytical techniques for quality control
   • Interpret analytical data
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Analytical chemistry fundamentals: classification, significance
   • Errors in analysis: types, calculation, statistical analysis
   • Gravimetric analysis: principles, calculations, applications
   • Volumetric analysis: titrimetry, indicators, end-point detection
   • Acid-base titrations: theory, calculations, standardization
   • Redox titrations: principles, applications in pharmacy
   • Complexometric titrations: EDTA titrations, masking agents
   • Precipitation titrations: Mohr, Volhard, Fajans methods
   • Official analysis methods: IP, BP, USP standards
   • Pharmaceutical applications and quality control

3. BP103T - PHARMACEUTICS I (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Explain pharmaceutical processes and unit operations
   • Design pharmaceutical formulations
   • Apply GMP and quality principles
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Introduction to pharmaceutics: definition, scope, role in pharmacy
   • Pharmaceutical processes: size reduction, size separation, mixing
   • Particle size and distribution: measurement, significance in formulation
   • Powders: characteristics, flow properties, density, porosity
   • Granulation: principles, methods (wet, dry), equipment
   • Drying: principles, types of dryers, drying kinetics
   • Extraction: principles, methods (maceration, percolation, distillation)
   • Filtration: types, equipment, filter media, sterile filtration
   • Introduction to dosage forms: classification and characteristics
   • GMP fundamentals for pharmaceutical manufacturing

4. BP104T - PHARMACEUTICAL INORGANIC CHEMISTRY (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Explain properties of inorganic pharmaceutical substances
   • Apply chemical principles to pharmaceutical inorganic compounds
   • Ensure quality and stability of inorganic drugs
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Atomic structure: Bohr's model, orbitals, electronic configuration
   • Chemical bonding: ionic, covalent, coordinate, hydrogen bonds
   • Periodic table: periodicity, trends in properties, classification
   • Inorganic pharmaceuticals: calcium salts, iron preparations, iodides
   • Alkalies and alkaline earth metals: properties, pharmaceutical uses
   • Halogens: pharmaceutical applications of fluorine, chlorine, iodine
   • Electrolytes and non-electrolytes: colligative properties
   • Complexes and coordination compounds: pharmaceutical applications
   • Dosage forms of inorganic drugs: solutions, suspensions, injections
   • Quality control and standardization of inorganic pharmaceuticals

5. BP105T - COMMUNICATION SKILLS (Theory)
   Credits: 2 | L: 2 | Max Hours: 30
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Develop professional communication skills
   • Apply effective written and oral communication
   • Enhance interpersonal and presentation skills
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Communication fundamentals: process, barriers, types
   • Professional communication in pharmacy
   • Written communication: reports, letters, emails, documentation
   • Oral communication: presentation, public speaking, group discussion
   • Interpersonal communication: active listening, empathy
   • Nonverbal communication: body language, eye contact
   • Patient counselling: techniques, communication with prescribers
   • Business communication and pharmacist-patient relationship
   • Communication in healthcare teams
   • Digital communication and professional etiquette

6. BP106RBT/RMT - REMEDIAL BIOLOGY/MATHEMATICS (Theory) *Optional
   Credits: 2 | L: 2 | Max Hours: 30
   • For students requiring remedial support

3. BP203T - PHARMACEUTICS II (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):

   • Evaluate physicochemical properties
   • Apply stability principles in formulation
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Liquid dosage forms: classification, formulation principles
   • Solutions: preparation, stability, isotonicity, tonicity
   • Suspensions: classification, preparation, evaluation
   • Emulsions: types, preparation, stability, evaluation
   • Colloids: properties, coagulation, flocculation
   • Semi-solid dosage forms: bases, classification
   • Ointments: formulation, application, stability
   • Creams: preparation, types (O/W, W/O), evaluation
   • Gels: types, formulation, rheological properties
   • Pastes: formulation, characteristics
   • Evaluation of semi-solids: spread-ability, consistency, appearance
   • Stability studies and shelf-life determination

4. BP204T - PHARMACEUTICAL ORGANIC CHEMISTRY I (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Apply organic chemistry principles to drug design
   • Explain reaction mechanisms relevant to pharmacy
   • Correlate structure with pharmacological activity
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Nomenclature: IUPAC rules, common names
   • Isomerism: structural, geometric, optical isomerism
   • Reaction mechanisms: SN1, SN2, E1, E2 reactions
   • Alkanes and cycloalkanes: properties, reactions
   • Alkenes and alkynes: properties, addition reactions, polymerization
   • Aromatic compounds: benzene structure, aromaticity
   • Electrophilic aromatic substitution: mechanism, directing effects
   • Nucleophilic aromatic substitution: mechanisms
   • Alcohols: classification, properties, reactions, oxidation
   • Aldehydes and ketones: properties, reactions, condensation
   • Carboxylic acids and derivatives: preparation, reactions
   • Pharmaceutical applications of organic compounds

5. BP205T - BIOCHEMISTRY (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Explain biochemical processes and metabolic pathways
   • Apply biochemical knowledge in pharmaceutical sciences
   • Understand enzyme kinetics and regulation
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Proteins: structure, amino acids, peptide bonds, classification
   • Protein folding and structure determination
   • Carbohydrates: classification, monosaccharides, disaccharides
   • Polysaccharides: starch, glycogen, cellulose
   • Carbohydrate metabolism: glycolysis, citric acid cycle, gluconeogenesis
   • Pentose phosphate pathway: significance, products
   • Lipids: classification, fatty acids, triglycerides, phospholipids
   • Lipid metabolism: β-oxidation, fatty acid synthesis
   • Ketone body metabolism and utilization
   • Cholesterol synthesis and regulation
   • Nucleic acids: DNA, RNA, structure, replication
   • Gene expression: transcription, translation, genetic code
   • Enzyme kinetics: Michaelis-Menten equation
   • Enzyme regulation: allosteric, covalent modification, feedback inhibition
   • Bioenergetics: ATP, energy coupling, oxidative phosphorylation

1. BP301T - PATHOPHYSIOLOGY (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Explain disease mechanisms and pathological processes
   • Correlate pathophysiology with drug therapy
   • Apply knowledge for therapeutic interventions
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Pathophysiology fundamentals: homeostasis, adaptation
   • Cell injury: causes, types, reversible and irreversible damage
   • Inflammation: acute and chronic, mediators, resolution
   • Fever: pyrexia, pyrogens, mechanism, consequences
   • Hypersensitivity reactions: types I-IV
   • Immune response: primary, secondary, memory
   • Immunodeficiency: AIDS, combined immunodeficiency
   • Neoplasia: benign vs malignant, classification, carcinogenesis
   • Atherosclerosis: pathogenesis, risk factors, progression
   • Hypertension: etiology, mechanisms, complications
   • Heart failure: systolic and diastolic, compensatory mechanisms
   • Respiratory diseases: COPD, asthma, pneumonia pathophysiology
   • Renal pathophysiology: acute kidney injury, chronic kidney disease
   • Diabetes mellitus: type 1 and 2, pathophysiology, complications
   • Liver diseases: cirrhosis, hepatitis, failure
   • Neurological disorders: stroke, Alzheimer's, Parkinson's

2. BP302T - PHARMACEUTICAL ORGANIC CHEMISTRY II (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Synthesize and analyze pharmaceutical organic compounds
   • Apply structure-activity relationships in drug design
   • Explain metabolic transformations of drugs
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Heterocyclic compounds: five and six-membered rings
   • Aromatic heterocycles: pyrrole, pyridine, indole, benzofuran
   • Pyrimidines and purines: structure, reactivity, nucleoside analogs
   • Reduction reactions: LiAlH4, NaBH4, hydrogenation
   • Oxidation reactions: permanganate, chromic acid, Jones oxidation
   • Grignard reactions and organometallic compounds
   • Wittig reaction and olefination
   • Friedel-Crafts reactions: acylation, alkylation
   • Synthesis of aromatic compounds: aniline derivatives
   • Synthesis of pharmaceutical compounds: acetaminophen, aspirin
   • Stereoselective synthesis and asymmetric synthesis
   • Structure-activity relationships (SAR) in drug design
   • Conformational analysis and drug design

3. BP303T - BIOCHEMISTRY II (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Explain advanced biochemical processes
   • Apply molecular biology techniques
   • Understand gene expression and regulation
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Advanced enzyme kinetics: competitive inhibition, non-competitive
   • Regulatory enzymes and allosteric regulation
   • Bioenergetics: free energy, entropy, thermodynamics
   • Oxidative phosphorylation: electron transport chain, ATP synthesis
   • Photosynthesis: light and dark reactions
   • Amino acid metabolism: transamination, decarboxylation, catabolism
   • Urea cycle: nitrogen disposal, disorders
   • Porphyrin metabolism: heme synthesis, degradation
   • Integration of metabolic pathways: fed and fasted states
   • Hormonal regulation of metabolism: insulin, glucagon, cortisol
   • Signal transduction: G-proteins, tyrosine kinase, second messengers
   • Gene expression: chromatin structure, transcription factors
   • Regulation of transcription and translation
   • Recombinant DNA technology: cloning, PCR, expression vectors
   • DNA sequencing and genome analysis
   • Biotechnology applications in pharmacy

4. BP304T - PHARMACOLOGY I (Theory)
   Credits: 4 | L-T: 3-1 | Max Hours: 45
   COURSE OUTCOMES (CO):
   • Explain pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics
   • Apply principles of drug action to therapeutic use
   • Understand adverse effects and drug interactions
   
   KEY TOPICS:
   • Pharmacology fundamentals: definition, scope, history
   • Routes of administration: oral, parenteral, inhalation, topical
   • Drug absorption: mechanisms, factors affecting absorption
   • Distribution: protein binding, volume of distribution, barriers
   • Metabolism: phase I, II, III reactions, enzyme induction, inhibition
   • Elimination: renal clearance, hepatic metabolism, half-life
   • Bioavailability and bioequivalence
   • Pharmacokinetic models: one and two compartment models
   • Drug-receptor interactions: agonists, antagonists, partial agonists
   • Dose-response relationships: EC50, TD50, therapeutic index
   • Autonomic nervous system: sympathetic, parasympathetic
   • Adrenergic drugs: α and β agonists, antagonists, clinical uses
   • Cholinergic drugs: muscarinic, nicotinic agents
   • Adverse effects: types, mechanisms, toxicity
   • Drug interactions: pharmacokinetic and pharmacodynamic
   • Individual variation in drug response

   KEY TOPICS:

   • Healthcare systems: primary, secondary, tertiary care
   • Community pharmacy: organization, services, patient care

   • Pharmaceutical care: principles, implementation, outcomes
   • Public health and pharmacy: epidemiology, disease prevention
   • Epidemiology basics: disease patterns, transmission, control
   • Communicable diseases: TB, malaria, dengue, COVID-19
   • Non-communicable diseases: diabetes, hypertension, obesity
   • Drug utilization studies: patterns, appropriateness, outcomes
   • Pharmacoeconomics: cost-effectiveness, QALY, cost-benefit analysis
   • Health policy and regulation in pharmacy
   • Pharmacy and society: roles in healthcare delivery
   • Ethics in pharmacy practice
   • Patient education and counselling services
