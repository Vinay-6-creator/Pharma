Common Drugs Reference

Quick reference for commonly used pharmaceutical drugs

🔴 Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)
Analgesic & Antipyretic
Safe in pregnancy
Hepatotoxic in overdose
No GI irritation
Less anti-inflammatory effect
🟠 Ibuprofen (NSAIDs)
Anti-inflammatory & Analgesic
Strong anti-inflammatory
GI irritation & ulcers
Fast pain relief
Renal toxicity risk
🔴 Aspirin
Antiplatelet, Analgesic, Anti-inflammatory
Prevents blood clots
Bleeding risk
Anti-inflammatory effect
GI upset common
💊 Amoxicillin
Antibiotic (Beta-lactam)
Broad-spectrum coverage
Allergic reactions possible
Well-tolerated orally
Resistance developing
💊 Ciprofloxacin
Antibiotic (Fluoroquinolone)
Excellent GI penetration
Tendon rupture risk
Gram-negative coverage
CNS side effects
🩺 Metformin
Antidiabetic (Biguanide)
First-line diabetes therapy
Lactic acidosis risk (rare)
Weight neutral/loss
GI side effects common
💓 Atorvastatin
Statin (Lipid-lowering)
Potent cholesterol reduction
Muscle myopathy risk
CV risk reduction proven
Liver monitoring needed
💓 Lisinopril
ACE Inhibitor (Antihypertensive)
Cardioprotective effect
Dry cough common
Renal protection
Hyperkalemia risk
🔴 Omeprazole
Proton Pump Inhibitor (Gastric acid suppression)
Excellent acid control
Hypomagnesemia with long-term use
GERD relief
Reduced mineral absorption
🧠 Fluoxetine
SSRI (Antidepressant)
Effective for depression/anxiety
Sexual dysfunction common
Long half-life (flexible dosing)
Serotonin syndrome risk
💓 Metoprolol
Beta-blocker (Antihypertensive)
Post-MI cardioprotection
Bronchospasm in asthmatics
HR/BP control
Fatigue and ED
💓 Digoxin
Cardiac Glycoside (Arrhythmia)
Dual action (inotropic + chronotropic)
Narrow therapeutic window
Long half-life (convenient)
Frequent toxicity/monitoring needed
🩺 Levothyroxine
Thyroid Hormone Replacement
Stable, once-daily dosing
Slow onset (weeks needed)
Physiologic replacement
Absorption affected by food/drugs
🩸 Warfarin
Anticoagulant (Vitamin K antagonist)
Potent anticoagulation
Bleeding risk significant
Oral form available
INR monitoring required
💙 Sildenafil
PDE5 Inhibitor (Erectile dysfunction/PAH)
Fast onset (30-60 min)
Headache and flushing common
Well-tolerated generally
Contraindicated with nitrates
