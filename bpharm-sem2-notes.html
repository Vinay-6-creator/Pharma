Hybridization: sp, sp², sp³ hybridization and molecular geometry

Alkanes, Alkenes & Alkynes: Structure, nomenclature, and reactivity patterns

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Draw the structure and explain sp³ hybridization in methane (CH₄)
Answer: In methane, carbon undergoes sp³ hybridization forming 4 sigma bonds with hydrogen atoms in a tetrahedral geometry. Each C-H bond is formed by overlap of sp³ hybrid orbital with 1s orbital of hydrogen.
Q2. Name the compound: CH₃-CH₂-CH₂-CH₃
Answer: This is a 4-carbon straight chain alkane = Butane (n-butane)

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Give IUPAC name for: CH₃-CHOH-CH₂-CHO
Answer: 3-hydroxybutanal (Number from aldehyde end to give lowest number to CHO group)
Q2. Identify the functional group in aspirin and explain its role
Answer: Aspirin contains carboxylic acid (-COOH) and ester (-COO-) groups. The carboxylic acid group enhances water solubility, while the ester group provides analgesic action.

Structural Isomerism: Chain, position, and functional group isomerism
•

•
Chirality: Chiral centers, enantiomers, racemic mixtures
•
Biological Importance: How isomerism affects drug activity and pharmaceutical effectiveness

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Draw structural isomers of C₄H₁₀O
Answer: Four isomers possible: 1-butanol, 2-butanol, 2-methyl-1-propanol, diethyl ether
Q2. Explain why D-glucose and L-glucose have different biological activities
Answer: The stereochemistry (3D spatial arrangement) affects how glucose molecules fit into enzyme active sites. Only D-glucose can fit properly into human enzyme binding sites for metabolism.

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Compare SN1 and SN2 mechanisms with examples
Answer:
SN1: Two-step mechanism, carbocation intermediate, unimolecular rate law, racemic mixture formation
SN2: One-step mechanism, no intermediate, bimolecular rate law, inversion of configuration, requires good nucleophile
Q2. What product forms when ethene reacts with HBr? Apply Markovnikov's rule
Answer: Product is bromoethane (CH₃-CH₂Br). According to Markovnikov's rule, H adds to the carbon with more H atoms and Br adds to the carbon with fewer H atoms.
