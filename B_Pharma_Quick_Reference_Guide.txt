B.PHARM SYLLABUS - QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE
=========================================

📌 PROGRAM STRUCTURE
====================
Duration: 8 Semesters (4 Years)
Credits: 208 Total
Attendance: 80% Minimum
Language: English

📚 SEMESTER BREAKDOWN
====================

SEM I (ODD) - 27-30 Credits
────────────────────────────
BP101T - Anatomy & Physiology I (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Human body, cells, tissues, cardiovascular, respiratory, nervous systems
  
BP102T - Pharmaceutical Analysis I (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Gravimetric analysis, volumetric analysis, titrimetry, acid-base titrations
  
BP103T - Pharmaceutics I (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Dosage forms, particle size, powders, granules, granulation
  
BP104T - Pharmaceutical Inorganic Chemistry (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Atomic structure, periodic table, inorganic pharmaceuticals
  
BP105T - Communication Skills (2L, 2Cr)
  → Professional & written communication, presentations

Practicals: Anatomy, Analysis, Pharmaceutics, Inorganic Chemistry


SEM II (EVEN) - 30 Credits
────────────────────────────
BP201T - Anatomy & Physiology II (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → GI system, endocrine, urinary, reproductive, immune systems
  
BP202T - Pharmaceutical Analysis II (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → UV-Vis spectroscopy, fluorimetry, chromatography, GC basics
  
BP203T - Pharmaceutics II (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Liquid dosage forms, solutions, suspensions, emulsions, semi-solids
  
BP204T - Organic Chemistry I (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Nomenclature, mechanisms, alkanes, aromatic compounds, functional groups
  
BP205T - Biochemistry I (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Carbohydrate, lipid, protein metabolism, nucleic acids, enzyme kinetics

Practicals: All 5 theory courses


SEM III (ODD) - 30 Credits
────────────────────────────
BP301T - Pathophysiology (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Disease mechanisms, inflammation, cardiovascular/respiratory/metabolic diseases
  
BP302T - Organic Chemistry II (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Heterocyclics, reduction, oxidation, pharmaceutical synthesis, SAR
  
BP303T - Biochemistry II (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Advanced kinetics, bioenergetics, signal transduction, gene expression
  
BP304T - Pharmacology I (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → General principles, pharmacokinetics, pharmacodynamics, ANS drugs
  
BP305T - Social Pharmacy (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Healthcare systems, community pharmacy, epidemiology, drug utilization

Practicals: Pathophysiology, Organic Chem II, Biochem II, Pharmacology I


SEM IV (EVEN) - 30 Credits
────────────────────────────
BP401T - Pharmacology II (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → CVS drugs, antihypertensives, diuretics, anti-arrhythmics
  
BP402T - Pharmaceutical Jurisprudence (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Pharmacy Act, Drugs & Cosmetics Act, IP, professional ethics
  
BP403T - Medicinal Chemistry I (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Drug discovery, lead identification, structure optimization, prodrugs
  
BP404T - Pharmacognosy I (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Plant ID, extraction, phytochemistry, medicinal plants, herbal evaluation
  
BP405T - Pharmaceutical Biotechnology (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Fermentation, recombinant DNA, biopharmaceuticals, bioassays

Practicals: Pharmacology II, Medicinal Chem I, Pharmacognosy I, Biotech


SEM V (ODD) - 30 Credits
────────────────────────────
BP501T - Pharmacology III (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → CNS drugs, antipsychotics, antidepressants, anxiolytics, anticonvulsants
  
BP502T - Medicinal Chemistry II (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Drug metabolism, phases, DDI, personalized medicine
  
BP503T - Pharmacognosy II (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Secondary metabolites, alkaloids, glycosides, terpenes, essential oils
  
BP504T - Pharmaceutics III (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Bioavailability, biopharmaceutics, formulation, advanced delivery
  
BP505T - Pharmaceutical Analysis III (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → HPLC, mass spectrometry, NMR, thermal analysis, method validation

Practicals: Pharmacology III, Medicinal Chem II, Pharmacognosy II, Pharma III


SEM VI (EVEN) - 30 Credits
────────────────────────────
BP601T - Pharmacology IV (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → GI, respiratory, antimicrobial, antiinfective, antiparasitic drugs
  
BP602T - Biopharmaceutics & Pharmacokinetics (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → ADME, absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion, models
  
BP603T - Pharmaceutical Microbiology (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Microorganisms, sterilization, disinfection, preservation, contamination
  
BP604T - Pharmaceutical Economics & Management (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Management, healthcare economics, cost analysis, pricing, insurance
  
BP605T - Regulatory Affairs (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Drug approval, GMP, quality standards, documentation, compliance

Practicals: BioPharm & PK, Microbiology, Economics & Management


SEM VII (ODD) - 30 Credits + 150 Hours Practice School
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BP701T - Pharmacology V (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Endocrine, hormonal, immunosuppressant, anti-inflammatory, chemotherapy
  
BP702T - Advanced Pharmacology (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Interactions, adverse reactions, toxicology, pharmacovigilance
  
BP703T - Pharmacy Practice (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Clinical pharmacy, TDM, drug info, patient counseling, error prevention
  
BP704T - Novel Drug Delivery Systems (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Nanoparticles, liposomes, microspheres, transdermal, targeted delivery
  
BP705T - Medicinal Chemistry III (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Antimicrobial, anticancer, cardiovascular synthesis, SAR, optimization

⭐ PRACTICE SCHOOL (150 Hours - 4 Credits):
   Choose ONE:
   • Hospital Pharmacy
   • Community Pharmacy
   • Clinical Research Organization
   • Research Organization

Practicals: Pharmacology V, Pharmacy Practice, Novel Delivery


SEM VIII (EVEN) - 30 Credits + Research Project
────────────────────────────────────────────────
BP801T - Pharmaceutical Research (2L, 4Cr)
  → Methodology, planning, data analysis, scientific writing, presentation
  
BP802T - Biopharmaceutics & PK (Advanced) (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Nonlinear PK, DDI, population PK, bioequivalence, clinical trials
  
BP803T - Pharmaceutical Product Development (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → Development process, formulation, optimization, stability, tech transfer
  
BP804T - Drug Regulatory Affairs (3L+1T, 4Cr)
  → CDSCO, WHO guidelines, ICH, dossiers, clinical trial regulations

ELECTIVE (Choose ONE) (3L+1T, 4Cr):
  • Pharmaceutical Product Development
  • Advanced Pharmaceutical Analysis
  • Cosmetics Formulation
  • Clinical Pharmacology
  • Pharmaceutical Entrepreneurship

⭐ CAPSTONE PROJECT (8-12 Credits):
   Research or Product Development
   Group project (Max 5 students)
   Dissertation work
   Internal & External Evaluation

Practicals: Research, Elective


✅ KEY REGULATIONS
==================
→ 80% Attendance Mandatory
→ Pass: 50% (40% with internal assessment)
→ I-II must be completed before III semester
→ III-IV must be completed before V semester
→ V-VI must be completed before VII semester
→ No "Odd Batch" allowed (must follow semester sequence)
→ Practice School: Mandatory 150 hours (Sem VII)
→ Research Project: Mandatory 8-12 Credits (Sem VIII)


📊 GRADING SYSTEM
==================
A+  = 90-100%  (Grade Point: 10)
A   = 80-89%   (Grade Point: 9)
B+  = 70-79%   (Grade Point: 8)
B   = 60-69%   (Grade Point: 7)
C   = 50-59%   (Grade Point: 6)
P   = 40-49%   (Grade Point: 5)
F   = <40%     (Grade Point: 0)
AB  = Absent   (Grade Point: 0)

SGPA = Σ(Credits × Grade Points) / Total Credits
CGPA = Overall GPA for entire program

  Evaluated by internal & external examiners
  Presentation & viva voce
