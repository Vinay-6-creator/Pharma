B Pharm - Semester 3

Pharmacology Introduction

Fundamentals of pharmacology including drug absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion (ADME).

I

ADME - Absorption

•
Absorption Pathways: Oral, transdermal, intramuscular, intravenous routes
•
Factors Affecting Absorption: pH, membrane permeability, molecular weight
•
Bioavailability: Definition and factors affecting drug bioavailability

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Define bioavailability and explain factors affecting it
Answer: Bioavailability is the fraction of administered drug that reaches systemic circulation. Factors include: route of administration, drug solubility, first-pass metabolism, and food effects.
II

ADME - Distribution & Metabolism

•
Drug Distribution: Plasma protein binding, volume of distribution
•
Metabolism: Phase I, II, III metabolic reactions
•
Excretion: Renal, hepatic, biliary excretion pathways

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Explain Phase I and Phase II drug metabolism with examples
Answer: Phase I (oxidation, reduction, hydrolysis) makes drugs more polar. Phase II (conjugation with glutathione, sulfate, glucuronic acid) makes them water-soluble for excretion.
III

Pharmacodynamics & Kinetics

•
Drug-Receptor Interactions: Agonists, antagonists, partial agonists
•
Dose-Response Relationship: ED50, TD50, Therapeutic Index
•
Pharmacokinetic Parameters: Clearance, half-life, Vd calculations

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. What is therapeutic index and its importance?
Answer: Therapeutic Index = TD50/ED50. Higher index means safer drug with wider margin between therapeutic and toxic doses.
IV

Drug Interactions & Adverse Effects

•
Types of Interactions: Synergism, antagonism, incompatibility
•
Adverse Drug Reactions: Classification and severity
•
Drug Toxicity: Acute, chronic, and cumulative toxicity

❓ Important Previous Year Questions

Q1. Differentiate between drug synergism and antagonism with pharmaceutical examples
Answer: Synergism: Combined effect > individual effects (e.g., ampicillin + sulbactam). Antagonism: Combined effect < individual effects or opposed actions.
